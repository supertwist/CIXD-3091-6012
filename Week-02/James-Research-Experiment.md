## ü§î Why some numbers differ across sources

*   **Stud height (1.7‚ÄØmm vs 1.8‚ÄØmm):** Brick Owl documents **1.7‚ÄØmm** (frequently measured on modern parts), while older or alternate measurements (and some drawings) show **1.8‚ÄØmm**; embossed **LEGO** text on solid studs and era/mold variations can add \~0.1‚ÄØmm. The StackExchange thread explicitly discusses these observed differences. [\[us-prod.as...rosoft.com\]](https://us-prod.asyncgw.teams.microsoft.com/v1/objects/0-cus-d14-8dc83f934349a7f277e0c775a9d6b354/views/original/lego_2x3_brick_drawing.pdf), [\[brickowl.com\]](https://www.brickowl.com/help/stud-dimensions)
*   **Pitch exactly 8.0‚ÄØmm vs measured \~7.986‚ÄØmm:** Design intent is **8.0‚ÄØmm**, but real parts often measure a hair under due to tooling tolerances and to guarantee assembly ‚Äúplay‚Äù (**0.1‚ÄØmm** per side), which is also why overall L/W are **0.2‚ÄØmm** under neat multiples of 8‚ÄØmm. [\[brickowl.com\]](https://www.brickowl.com/help/stud-dimensions)
*   **Underside tube OD:** You may see **‚âà6.5‚ÄØmm** in engineering drawings (e.g., **6.51‚ÄØmm** in the BU sheet) versus other hobby pages rounding differently; wall thicknesses are likewise reported as **1.5‚Äì1.6‚ÄØmm** depending on source, part age, or rounding policy. I used **6.51‚ÄØmm** and **1.5‚ÄØmm** to match the detailed engineering reference. [\[bricks.sta...change.com\]](https://bricks.stackexchange.com/questions/288/what-are-the-dimensions-of-a-lego-brick)

These differences are normal and stem from **manufacturing tolerance, mold era changes, rounding conventions, and measurement method (design spec vs. caliper on real parts).** [\[brickowl.com\]](https://www.brickowl.com/help/stud-dimensions)
